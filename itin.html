What is an ITIN?

An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a tax processing number issued by the IRS to individuals who are required to file a U.S. tax return but are not eligible for a Social Security Number.

Who Needs an ITIN?

  • Non-resident individuals required to file a U.S. tax return
  • Spouses or dependents of U.S. citizens or residents
  • Dependents living in Mexico or Canada
  • Foreign nationals with U.S. tax obligations
  • Individuals needing to claim certain tax credits

Required Documentation

The IRS requires proof of identity and foreign status. The most commonly used document is:

  • Original passport (preferred), OR
  • Two alternative identification documents (birth certificate, national ID, etc.).

Additional documents may be required depending on the category of the applicant.

The Application Process

  1. Schedule an ITIN appointment
  2. Bring your identification documents
  3. CAA verifies your identity
  4. Form W-7 is completed with required attachments
  5. Your application package is sent to the IRS
  6. ITIN processing typically takes 7–11 weeks
